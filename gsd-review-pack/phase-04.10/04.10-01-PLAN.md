# Plan 04.10-01: Define The Hybrid Substrate Adapter And Ownership Seam

## Objective

Define the architectural seam that lets Core2 use a MemPalace-inspired substrate without surrendering ownership of truth/state or answer behavior.

## Must Haves

- single-owner rules remain explicit
- clear adapter boundary between substrate and truth layers
- no ambiguous dual truth engine
- explicit entry points for storage and retrieval

## Tasks

1. Identify the concrete Core2 insertion points for:
   - raw/verbatim storage
   - scoped retrieval
2. Decide the bounded adapter shape:
   - internal service
   - module seam
   - or provider-internal substrate layer
3. Write a short design artifact that names:
   - what Core2 continues to own
   - what the hybrid substrate owns
   - what must never cross the seam
   - the tiny stable interface exposed upward to Core2
4. Record cutover policy:
   - shadow first
   - bounded cutover
   - or another tightly scoped equivalent

## Verification

- The adapter seam is describable in a few sentences.
- Truth/state and answer-surface ownership remain unambiguous.
- The phase has clear insertion points rather than vague “we’ll integrate it somehow”.
- The substrate interface is small enough that MemPalace-specific internal details do not leak upward.
