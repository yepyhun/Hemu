# Plan 04.9-03: Derive The Follow-On Branch Decision And Stop Rules

## Objective

Turn the codemap and adoption matrix into a small number of credible next-step branches instead of an open-ended architecture wishlist.

## Must Haves

- Follow-on work is conditional, not automatic.
- Branches are mutually understandable:
  - keep Core2 and borrow little
  - build a bounded hybrid
  - replace one bounded layer
- Each branch has:
  - trigger conditions
  - forbidden moves
  - expected upside
  - complexity ceiling

## Tasks

1. Write `MEMPALACE-ROADMAP-OPTIONS.md` with at least these branches:
   - `Core2-first`
   - `Hybrid substrate`
   - `Bounded layer replacement`
2. For each branch, define:
   - when it is justified
   - what evidence would falsify it
   - what later phase(s) it would require
3. Record explicit stop rules:
   - no automatic 04.10+ commitment
   - no wholesale MemPalace replacement without a later decision phase
   - no “best of both worlds” branch unless ownership stays simple
4. Recommend the next GSD move after 04.9:
   - broader paid rerun first
   - or first implementation borrowing phase
   - or no follow-on until more evidence exists

## Verification

- `MEMPALACE-ROADMAP-OPTIONS.md` exists.
- The follow-on branches are conditional and bounded.
- The recommended next step is explicit and does not assume that all future phases are mandatory facts.
